Project Overview

The project analyzes the Olist e-commerce dataset to explore sales trends and delivery performance. It aims to uncover actionable insights that can support better decision-making in sales strategies and logistics operations.

The analysis is intended for the sales and logistics teams. Sales managers can use the insights on order patterns and sales trends to refine their strategies, while logistics teams can leverage delivery performance analysis to optimize operations and reduce delays.

Objective

To analyze order patterns and delivery performance in order to identify trends, inefficiencies, and opportunities for improving sales and logistics processes.

Context

Founded in 2014, Olist is a Brazilian e-commerce platform that connects small businesses across the country to multiple sales channels through a single contract. Merchants list their products in the Olist Store and ship orders directly to customers using Olist‚Äôs logistics partners. After a purchase is made, sellers are notified to fulfill the order. Once the product is delivered‚Äîor the estimated delivery date has passed‚Äîthe customer receives a satisfaction survey via email, where they can rate their shopping experience and leave feedback.

Role

Data Analyst ‚Äì responsible for data cleaning, exploratory data analysis (EDA), and creating visualizations to extract and communicate insights.

Tools and Methodologies

Python, Pandas, Matplotlib/Seaborn, Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA), Data Visualization, Statistics

Insights

Sales

  • Demand was low at the end of 2016, then rose steadily through 2017 with clear seasonality, peaking at about 1,200 daily orders on Black Friday, before flattening in 2018 at around 6,500 monthly orders.
  • Operational performance improved sharply, as the Cancelled Rate fell from 8% in 2016 to 1% in 2017‚Äì2018, the Unavailable Rate declined from 2% to 0%, and the Fulfillment Rate increased from 81% to 98%.
  • Revenue also grew strongly, with GMV reaching 5.96M in 2017 and already surpassing that in just the first eight months of 2018 at 7.22M, averaging 0.8‚Äì0.9M per month.
  • The Average Order Value ranged between $120‚Äì150, spiking to about $337, while weekday afternoons (especially Monday‚ÄìWednesday) drove the highest order volumes, and Thursday evenings and Friday afternoons recorded the highest AOV.

Logistics

  • Olist shows strong delivery performance, with an average Delivery Lead Time of 10 days, including 1‚Äì4 days inbound and 4‚Äì12 days outbound. This balance keeps the overall delivery process efficient.
  • The on-time delivery rate is high: 90.4% for customers and 82.4% for carriers. Only 9.6% of orders were late, mainly caused by carriers (62.6%) rather than sellers (37.8%).
  • Deliveries made on time are associated with higher customer review scores, highlighting the importance of punctual logistics.
  • Geographically, most customers are in the east, while sellers are concentrated in the southeast. Delivery lead time is longest in the northwest but shortest in the southeast, where Olist‚Äôs GMV is also highly concentrated.

Recommendations

  • Olist should upsell or launch new products to increase order volume during weekdays in the afternoon (12h‚Äì17h).
  • Olist should promote higher-priced products on Thursday evenings and Friday afternoons, for example through Premium Brand Days or Golden Hour campaigns.
  • Afternoon peak hours (see slide 22) are ideal for pushing promotions via banners, notifications, flash sales, or calls to action.
  • Olist should keep Delivery Lead Time short in the southeast to sustain the largest GMV, while also improving lead times in distant states‚Äîpotentially by creating regional hubs to reduce cost.
  • Olist should collect additional datasets, including:
    • Distance data to analyze routes and improve delivery speed.
    • Discount and promotion data to assess their impact on GMV.
    • User demographic data to better understand customers and drive higher GMV.
