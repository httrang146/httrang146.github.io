Project Overview

This project involved developing a comprehensive and interactive Power BI Inventory Tracking Dashboard to monitor and analyze inventory performance. The dashboard enables stakeholders to quickly assess inventory health, understand sales trends, track fluctuations in Sell-in prices, manage vendors, and make informed replenishment decisions.

By simulating inventory management scenarios using sample retail data, the project demonstrates practical insights for supply chain optimization.

Objective

  • To monitor inventory levels in real-time and ensure optimal stock availability.
  • To identify understocked and overstocked products for better inventory control.
  • To analyze Sell-in and Sell-out trends to support data-driven sales and replenishment decisions.
  • To track fluctuations in Sell-in prices and assess their impact on inventory and profitability.
  • To manage vendor performance and ensure timely deliveries.
  • To provide actionable insights that help optimize overall supply chain operations.

Context

The dashboard was built using the Inventory Analysis Case Study dataset by Bhanupratap Biswas, which simulates a real-world retail inventory environment. The data consists of three main tables:

  • EndInvFINAL12312016: Provides a snapshot of stock levels as of December 31, 2016, helping to assess inventory at a specific point in time.
  • PurchasesFINAL12312016: Records all purchase orders, including SKU-level details and receiving dates, enabling analysis of procurement and vendor performance.
  • SalesFINAL12312016: Contains sales transactions over the year, reflecting customer demand and facilitating Sell-in and Sell-out trend analysis.

Role

As a Data Analyst, I was responsible for designing and developing the Inventory Tracking Dashboard to support the SCM Operations team. My tasks included:

  • Data preparation and cleaning: Consolidating and transforming data from multiple tables (EndInvFINAL12312016, PurchasesFINAL12312016, SalesFINAL12312016) to ensure accuracy and consistency.
  • Dashboard design and development: Creating interactive Power BI visuals, KPIs, and metrics such as stock turnover, out-of-stock rate, and demand coverage.
  • Interactivity and analysis: Implementing slicers, filters, and drill-through functionality to allow the team to explore inventory trends, vendor performance, and Sell-in/Sell-out fluctuations.
  • Stakeholder collaboration: Working closely with SCM Operations teammates to tailor the dashboard to their operational needs and ensure actionable insights were easily accessible.

    • This role allowed me to combine data analysis, visualization, and business understanding to provide a practical tool for supply chain optimization.

Tools and Methodologies

  • Tools: Power BI (visualization, DAX), Excel (data preparation and transformation), sample datasets for Sell-in, Sell-out, and inventory.
  • Methodologies: Data modeling and transformation, KPI definition, interactive dashboard design, inventory performance analysis, and scenario-based insights.
